Concerntration

Very often, when we talk about ‘concentration’ in relation to our studies we really mean ‘attention’. Are you always paying attention? Are you fully engaged with your studies? When we multitask, for instance, we give only brief attention to one thing and then to another. What it does is effectively tire us out, because attention is a limited resource. If we follow the logic that ‘attention is limited’ we can be mindful of needing to be fully invested, mentally, in one thing at a time to get the fullest out of it.

Concentration is the ability to direct one’s attention in accordance with one’s will. It means control of the attention. It is the ability to focus the mind on one subject, object or thought without being distracted to complete its task.

Tips for improving concentration:-

  • Reduce distractions - don’t have your mobile phone out while studying.
  • Have clearly defined targets.
  • Relax and stay calm.
  • Avoid too much stimulation, for example coffee or playing video games before study.
